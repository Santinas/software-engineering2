Cookie Policy

How iCreate uses cookies and local storage.

Last updated: July 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how iCreate uses cookies and similar browser storage technologies to keep the platform working and remember your session.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies and browser storage (such as localStorage and sessionStorage) are small pieces of data saved in your browser. They let a website remember information between pages and visits — for example, keeping you logged in.

2. How We Use Them

iCreate uses only essential, functional storage. We do not use advertising or third-party tracking cookies.

PurposeWhat it does
AuthenticationKeeps you securely signed in to your account (managed by Supabase).
"Remember me"Chooses whether your session persists after you close the browser, based on your login preference.
Session & preferencesRemembers your display name, role, and read/unread notification state so the site works smoothly.
SecurityCloudflare Turnstile may set storage to verify you are a real person and prevent abuse.

3. Managing Cookies

You can clear or block cookies and browser storage in your browser settings at any time. Please note that disabling essential storage will prevent you from staying logged in and may stop parts of iCreate from working correctly.

4. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Continued use of iCreate after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
