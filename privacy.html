Privacy Policy

How iCreate collects, uses, and protects your information.

Last updated: July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information iCreate collects and how we use it. By using the platform, you consent to the practices described below.

1. Information We Collect

  • Account information: your name, email address, and password (passwords are securely handled by our authentication provider and never stored in plain text).
  • Profile information: for freelancers — program, headline, bio, skills, rate, contact details, and portfolio files you choose to upload.
  • Commission details: project descriptions, budgets, and any payment proof you submit.
  • Messages: the content of chats and any files you send through the platform.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To create and manage your account and authenticate logins.
  • To display freelancer profiles on the public marketplace.
  • To connect clients and freelancers for commissions and messaging.
  • To send account-related emails such as password resets.

3. Who Can See Your Information

Freelancer profiles are public on the marketplace. Chat messages and commission details are visible only to the parties involved and platform administrators. We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

4. Third-Party Services

iCreate relies on trusted providers to operate: Supabase for authentication and database storage, Cloudflare Turnstile for bot protection, and optionally Google for sign-in. These providers process data on our behalf under their own privacy terms.

5. Data Retention

We keep your information for as long as your account is active. Freelancer profiles may be removed by administrators, and you may request deletion of your account and associated data.

6. Your Choices

You can update your profile information at any time and request that your account be removed. For account or data requests, contact the iCreate team through iAcademy.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Continued use of iCreate after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

See also our Terms of Service and Cookie Policy.
